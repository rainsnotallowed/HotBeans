Resources/Info

Here are some resources that can help you learn more about web development and stay up to date with the latest trends and technologies in the industry:

  • W3Schools

    • W3 schools is a great resource for learning web development basics.

  • MDN Web Docs

    • MDN Web Docs provide comprehensive documentation for web developers.

  • freeCodeCamp

    • freeCodeCamp offers interactive coding challenges and projects to help you learn web development.

  • CSS-Tricks

    • CSS-Tricks is a popular website that provides tips, tricks, and tutorials for CSS and front-end development.

  • Smashing Magazine

    • Smashing Magazine is a great resource for web designers and developers, offering articles, tutorials, and resources on a wide range of topics related to web development.

information in on the roles

Developer

What They Do: Developers write, test, and maintain code to build web applications and software. They work on both front-end (user interface) and back-end (server-side logic) systems, ensuring applications run smoothly and efficiently.

Qualifications Needed:

Strong knowledge of programming languages (JavaScript, Python, Java, etc.)

Understanding of web technologies (HTML, CSS, frameworks)

Problem-solving and debugging skills

Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field (or equivalent experience)

Experience with version control systems (Git)

Knowledge of databases and APIs

Designer

What They Do: Designers create the visual appearance and user experience of websites and applications. They design layouts, color schemes, and interfaces to make products both beautiful and easy to use. They work closely with developers to bring their designs to life.

Qualifications Needed:

Proficiency in design tools (Adobe XD, Figma, Sketch, Photoshop)

Strong understanding of UX/UI principles

Knowledge of web design trends and best practices

Portfolio demonstrating design work

Basic understanding of HTML/CSS

Communication and collaboration skills

Degree in Graphic Design or related field (or equivalent experience)

Manager

What They Do: Managers oversee teams, coordinate projects, and ensure that deadlines are met and quality standards are maintained. They handle resource planning, team communication, and strategic direction for the organization or project.

Qualifications Needed:

Leadership and team management experience

Strong organizational and planning skills

Knowledge of project management methodologies (Agile, Scrum, Waterfall)

Excellent communication and interpersonal skills

Bachelor's degree in Business Management or related field

3+ years of experience in project or team management

Understanding of web development or software industry
